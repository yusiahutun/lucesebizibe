Britain's naughtiest Xmas party knees-up revealed...and it's in NORWICH

January 2024 · 2 minute read

Sugar & Spice currently sits in second place on the World's Best Strip Clubs' list – behind only the New Orleans Club in Warsaw, Poland.

And for this year's festivities, the East Anglia joint has been hosting parties across the season – where the dancers are all dressed in Mrs Claus outfits.

According to its website, it promises to show "amazing stage shows", "private and exclusive luxury VIP bed-rooms" and "girl-on-girl private shows" over the period.

Owner and actor Daz Crawford, who performed as Diesel in the hit ITV show Gladiators, told Daily Star Online: "The girls at the moment are all dressed as sexy Santas.

"We have the money guns. You can load it with money, with notes, so you can put a few hundred in and fire them off.

"They make it rain, they make money rain. It's a term within the industry, a customer might come in and just blast the cash – notes everywhere."

It was launched by 49-year-old Crawford – who said he was keen on loosening the stigma attached to the industry – in 2010.

A former stripper himself, Crawford said he wanted the venue to be heavily influenced by clubs in US and sets out to provide "American Style Table Dancing".

Crawford told us: "I used to be a stripper years ago with the Dream Boys, and I used to do my own shows, I used to date a girl at Stringfellows.

"I always said, it has to be different, I know a lot about the stripping world and I knew about the stigma that came with industry where it is negative.

"With my ex-girlfriend being at Stringfellows, I heard a lot of stories about what went on and I didn't like it.

"I have a couple of friends in LA and they have got table dancing clubs and I knew the atmosphere in there was very, very different.

"They encourage girls, they have female customers, everybody is welcome, there's no stigma.

"There are some dirty ones but it is a lot cleaner generally, and it was just nicer. At clubs in the States a lot of the customers are female, and in the UK it was not like that it was always known as a guy thing, some clubs didn't even let girls in."
