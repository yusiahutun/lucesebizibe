Who are the couples on Life Below Zero: Next Generation?

August 2024 · 7 minute read

Life Below Zero: Next Generation has proven to be an excellent educational TV series on the National Geographic network. It takes viewers on a beautiful tour of Alaska as they follow the stories of individuals who have left their traditional lifestyles to face the challenging new world of living in the Alaskan wilds. In addition, the new generation of people aged between 25 and 45, some of whom are couples, branch out on a journey of self-discovery. But who are the couples on Life Below Zero: Next Generation?

Life Below Zero: Next Generation premiered in 2020 and brought together a diverse ensemble of people who were not happy with the dullness of everyday life in a society driven by technology.

The couples in the television series venture into the risky and entirely unknown territory of the Alaskan wilderness. They have no experience but each other to survive. However, each one of them has a unique aspect of what it means to survive.

The couples in Next Generation are not as seasoned as those in other Life Below Zero franchises. But that is the whole point of the series. As an audience, you get to watch them fail, mess up, grow and learn.

Who are the couples on Life Below Zero: Next Generation?

Next Generation has three couples only. But the television show also has other individuals who are part of the cast. The couples and Life Below Zero: Next Generation cast members are:

1. Kaleb Rowland and Brittany Rowland

Kaleb and Brittany Rowland are the most notable millennial couples in Next Generation. Kaleb is an Alaskan native who grew up in McCarthy, an old mining city on the South of Kennecott Glacier. His wife, Brittany Rowland, was born in Fairbanks, Alaska, but lived in Anchorage.

Kaleb moved to Fairbanks to work as a commercial fisherman when he was 19 years old. However, he did not prefer city life and decided to go back to McCarthy. He met Brittany in 2011, and the two got married one year later, on 4 July 2012. Instead of moving to Fairbanks, the couple decided to remain in McCarthy and raise a family.

In Life Below Zero: Next Generation, Kaleb and Brittany have two young children, Gilbert and Elovie Rowland. The Rowland family has appeared in 11 episodes, but their kids have only made five appearances each.

2. Chris Morse and Jessi Morse

Chris and Jessi Morse are the youngest couple in Life Below Zero: Next Generation. The two were born in Alaska and met while in school. They have spent most of their lives in a cabin located on the outskirts of Fairbanks, Alaska.

Although Chris and Jessi prefer the wilderness life, they work in Fairbanks, where they do the regular nine-to-five job. However, the job is to help them save some money to live in a remote cabin along the Cosna River located about 100 miles away from their current location. Thus, they will have to live a life where they must hunt, trap, and fish for their survival.

Chris and Jessi have been together as a married couple for seven years. However, the pair does not have any children. They have both appeared in 13 episodes of Life Below Zero: Next Generation.

3. The Roach Family

The Roach family became part of the Life Below Zero cast from the third season. Chevie Roach is the Roach family’s patriarch, his wife is Sonta Hamilton, and he has three children (Emery, Ryder, and Sydney).

Chieve was born in Fairbanks, Alaska, and grew up in Tok. However, he moved to Shageluck after meeting his wife Sonta. Sonta Hamilton was born in Shageluck, Alaska, where her parents also live.

Chevie works as the mayor and has a local store called The Flyaway, while Sonta is a schoolteacher. Before Chevie became mayor, he used to be a tribal officer at Shageluck since the town only has 80 people, and people can only get in or out by plane or boat.

Although the Roach family have what people might call white-collar jobs, they rely on subsistence activities for their survival. For example, Chevie likes to fish and hunt.

4. Alex Javor

Alex Javor is an Army veteran born in Alabama. He moved to Alaska in 2015 and purchased a piece of land in Bear Creek, Alaska. The environment impressed him, and he decided to build a cabin off the ground.

But living in Alaska, especially in Bear Creek, has proven challenging to the army veteran. He has faced a situation where he ran out of essential resources to sustain him in the wild. As a result, he moved to a new location near Deadman Lake.

Despite moving to the remote area around Deadman Lake, Alex Javor must start afresh. That means he has to build a new house, get water and wood, and start fishing and hunting. However, his experience in the army has given him enough skills to tackle the challenges he faces in Alaska.

5. Michael Manzo

Michael Manzo moved to Alaska in 2002. He attended Alaska Pacific University and graduated in 2014 with a degree in sustainable studies. Before appearing in Life Below Zero: Next Generation, Michael worked in the US Army and was a team member of the 84th Engineers Combat Heavy Battalion.

Michael Manzo prefers fishing and hunting. However, he builds canoes and is the owner of Yukon Freightworks Canoe Company. During the winter, he depends on dog teams to hunt for prey. Furthermore, Michael prefers a sustainable life and tries to eliminate gasoline use.

Michael appeared in only 12 episodes of Next Generation and was subsequently absent during the third season.

6. Johnny Rolfe

Johnny Rolfe was born in Chesterton, Indiana, United States, and moved to Alaska to start his off-the-grid living. He has a small wooden cabin with no modern amenities and spends most of his time with Java's dog.

Johnny has only appeared in three episodes and has shown that he is a survivalist who can tackle the Alaskan wilderness.

What happened to the Life Below Zero cast?

At the beginning of the third season, audiences witnesses Life Below Zero cast changes whereby Michael Manzo was missing from the show. Michael left the series, and the producers replaced him with the Roach family. But Michael still lives in Alaska and uses his knowledge in sustainability to survive the Alaskan wilds.

Is Life Below Zero cancelled? Life Below Zero: Next Generation is not cancelled. The series continues in its third season in 2021. Furthermore, the Life Below Zero franchise is ongoing, with none of the shows under it cancelled.
