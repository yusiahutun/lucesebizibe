List of NGOs offering attachments in Kenya and how to apply

June 2024 · 8 minute read

College or university students attain their first job experience through attachments directly linked to their courses. NGOs offering attachments in Kenya give learners great job-hunting skills while equipping them with out-of-the-classroom knowledge.

Like most private or government-owned firms in Kenya, non-profit organizations offer internship opportunities too. However, these attachment programs can be competitive depending on the non-profit organization you are looking forward to working in.

List of NGOs offering attachments in Kenya

Securing a job with an NGO is a dream for many people. However, landing an internship with the organization is the first step towards making this dream come true. Here is a list of NGOs offering attachments in Kenya and how to apply:

1. RefuSHE

RefuSHE offers one of the best-paid NGO internships in Kenya. Headquartered in Nairobi, the organization works with refugee communities in East Africa. It offers a full spectrum of protection and care for vulnerable children and young women.

The NGO offers paid internships, with the amount varying from one position to another. Even though it's not mandatory, preferred interns should be holders of a bachelor's degree in:

  • Social Work
  • Sociology
  • Counselling Psychology

Internship opportunities are advertised on their official website, and application requirements are:

  • A cover letter
  • Detailed CV
  • Referee

2. Internal Rescue Committee

Internal Rescue Committee offer internships under various positions as the need arises. The NGO helps people to survive, recover, and rebuild their lives after facing conflict, war, or natural disasters. Interns applying for positions at the organization are expected to adapt to the dynamic duties and responsibilities as they emerge.

The NGO considers applicants on merit because it is an equal-opportunity employer. To apply, contact the firm's Talent Acquisition via IRC.Recruitment@rescue.org. All applicants are offered reasonable accommodations upon qualification.

3. One Acre Fund

One Acre Fund is one of Africa's biggest smallholder farmer's networks. It offers training and financing to smallholders to get rid of hunger and build permanent prosperity. The NGO launched a paid attachment program, the Young Professional Program, for recent African graduates. The stipend covers the following costs:

  • Transportation
  • Housing
  • Communication

Internship opportunities are opened twice a year, with the primary requirements being:

  • University graduates
  • Fluent in English
  • Fluent in the local language

Interested applicants should visit the NGO’s website for more information. Also, they are advised to go through the article, Four Tips for a Successful One Acre Fund Internship Application, published on the website.

4. United Nations

United Nations stands out as one of the biggest sources of internships and job vacancies in Kenya and globally. The internships are managed by the Staff Development and Training Unit (SDTU) on behalf of the following UN Organizations:

  • United Nations Dispute Tribunal, Office of Administration of Justice (OAJ)
  • United Nations Human Settlements Programme (UN-HABITAT)
  • United Nations Offices at Nairobi (UNON)
  • United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP)

To qualify for UN attachment opportunities in Kenya, you must:

  • Be enrolled in a graduate school program
  • Be enrolled in the final academic year of your first university degree programme
  • Have graduated with a university degree
  • If chosen, you must begin the internship within a year of graduation

Interested candidates should visit the United Nations Careers Portal to apply for the internship vacancies.

5. Kenya National Commission of Human Rights

The KNCHR promotes human rights, democratic values, human dignity, and social justice. The NGO has been offering attachment vacancies in various positions since 2003. These opportunities are opened and announced through:

  • Their official website
  • Daily newspapers
  • Partner organizations

To apply, interested applicants should write to internships@knchr.org.

6. AMREF Kenya

Does AMREF Kenya offer internships? Yes, the NGO offers local internship and volunteer opportunities. Even though interns are not full-time employees at the organization, they are expected to offer their knowledge, skills, and competencies while acquiring additional training.

Attachment opportunities are posted on the NGO’s internships page detailing:

  • The scope of work
  • Academic qualifications
  • Location
  • Internship duration

To apply, email the HR team at Internship-KCO@Amref.org and clearly state the position you are applying for in the subject line. Also, remember to attach your;

7. African Wildlife Foundation

The African Wildlife Foundation (AWF) is an NGO that protects wildlife and its natural resources in Africa by influencing management policies and conservation practices. Even though the organization does not have a specific attachment program, it offers various vacancies on its website.

Stipends are paid depending on the candidate's position. Those interested in internship opportunities at AWF are advised to check for vacancies on their official website. To apply, they should send their cover letter and CV as one file to the NGO's HR and Administration Manager.

8. World Vision Kenya

The World Vision is one of the best Christian humanitarian organizations in the country. The NGO is devoted to helping kids, communities, and families reach their full potential by tackling poverty and injustice causes.

The organization provides a Graduate Internship Program (GRIP) to young individuals interested in the NGO’s work, focusing on the following:

  • The latest research experience

9. World Wide Fund for Nature Kenya

WWF-Kenya offers some of the best NGO internships for undergraduates. The locally registered conservation organization is part of the World Wide Fund for Nature International (WWF). For more than five decades, the organization has been responsible for managing:

  • Freshwater resources
  • Marine resources
  • Forest’s conservation and restoration

The World Wide Fund for Nature Kenya provides various attachment vacancies to individuals between 19 and 27 years of age. The Young Adult Volunteer & Internship Programme is the most popular, 12-month paid assignment. The attachment vacancies are posted on the NGO’s current openings page.

To apply:

  • Fill in and submit the application form
  • If accepted, you will receive an email asking you to submit supporting documents
  • Once the documents have been reviewed, you will attend an interview on Zoom, Skype, or Google Hangouts
  • Once accepted into the internship programme, all the relevant information will be sent to you, including your assignment

10. SOS Children’s Villages Kenya

SOS Children’s Villages Kenya is one of the world’s largest NGOs that focuses on supporting kids without parental care. The organization is an affiliate of SOS CV- International. In the country, SOS Children operates in:

  • Nairobi
  • Mombasa
  • Eldoret
  • Meru
  • Kisumu

Different attachment opportunities are opened on the official website, usually for six months, depending on the needs. To apply, interested candidates should send their full application with an updated CV and cover letter. Also, they should have excellent;

  • English knowledge
  • Communication skills
  • Other competencies, depending on the position available

11. Justice Africa

Justice Africa is a research facility and advocacy organization focusing mainly on human rights and social justice. The NGO supports civil society through various activities while seeking to offer a voice to marginalized individuals and groups affected by conflicts in Africa.

The organization offers various unpaid attachment programs for young professionals, recent graduates, or currently enrolled students from colleges and universities worldwide. No professional experience is needed to secure the three to six months internship.

12. Refugee Consortium of Kenya

Refugee Consortium of Kenya (RCK) is an NGO that promotes a safe environment where the displaced and host populations can easily access justice and protection. The organization also ensures that they are empowered to achieve their full potential. The Kenyan National NGO aims to protect and promote the rights and dignity of:

  • Refugees
  • Asylum seekers
  • Displaced people in the country

The organization offers some of the best NGO internship opportunities in Kenya as needed. The vacancies are available to individuals with academic backgrounds in Law, Psychology and Social Work. Interested candidates should send their CV and cover letter to info@rckkenya.org to apply.
