Anna Duggar makes a surprising gesture as husband Josh Duggar attends child abuse trial

Josh has pled not guilty

    Anna Duggar walked into court hand-in-hand with her husband Josh on Tuesday for day one of his court trial.

    After a 2019 raid on his Arkansas car dealership, the father-of-seven willingly turned himself in on 29 April 2021.

    If convicted, Josh could receive 20 years in prison with fines up to $250,000 on each count.

    The trial began with jury selection, and a day prior an evidentiary hearing to discuss Josh's past molestation scandal was attended by Anna, his father Jim Bob Duggar and family friend Bobye Holt.

    The defense team has moved to exclude any mention of the accusations from the upcoming trial.

    Jim Bob, 56, claimed he couldn't remember the details of Josh's admissions although Bobye, who is married to Jim Holt, an elder in the Duggar's church, recalled that Josh had repeatedly admitted, in 2003 and in 2005, to inappropriately touching four Jane Does over and under their clothes.

    Josh is a father of seven

    Anna and Josh became parents for the seventh time on 23 October; the new mom shared a gorgeous picture of her daughter in November revealing the newborn was born weighing 7 pounds, 9 ounces, and measuring 20 inches.

    Anna and Josh are also parents to Mackynzie Renée, 12, Michael James, 10, Marcus Anthony, 8, Meredith Grace, 6, Mason Garrett, 4, and Maryella Hope, who will turn 2 later this month.
