Kate Middleton Embraces Summer in a Green Leopard-Print Sundress

May 2024 · 2 minute read

Catherine, Princess of Wales, paid a visit to the English town of Nuneaton today for a cause close to her heart.

The royal stopped by Riversley Park Children’s Centre, where she met with children, professionals, and health visitors taking part in a new field study—funded by her Royal Foundation Centre for Early Childhood—on infant well-being and social and emotional development, per a Kensington Palace note.

For her visit to the Nuneaton children’s clinic, Kate wore a breezy white sundress with an abstract green-and-blue “leopard pansy print” from Cefinn. The silk maxi features long sleeves and small black buttons at the center.

Kate paired it simply with white pointed-toe pumps, a matching white leather handbag, pear-shaped crystal earrings, and her sapphire-and-diamond engagement ring. Her makeup was natural, and her hair was styled in relaxed waves.

WPA Pool//Getty Images

In photos, she is seen holding a bouquet of white flowers while waving to fans, and chatting with groups of kids outside the clinic.

PHIL NOBLE//Getty Images

Since becoming a royal, Princess Kate has made children’s education, mental health, and early development priorities among the causes she supports. She founded the Royal Foundation Centre for Early Childhood in an effort to “drive awareness of, and action on, the transformative impact of the early years.”

Earlier this month, she took her Shaping Us campaign to Maidenhead Rugby Club, where she met with local and national male players to discuss the importance of early childhood development—which data shows men are still significantly less aware of than women.
