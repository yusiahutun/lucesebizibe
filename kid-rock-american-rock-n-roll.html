Is Kid Rock's 'American Rock 'n Roll' a Hit?

September 2024 Â· 3 minute read

Kid Rock is no stranger to the country genre. Having seen success with several country singles over the years, his latest effort "American Rock 'n Roll" blends the singer's love for country, rock and soul music.

On "American Rock 'n Roll," the singer paints the picture of a perfect night for two teenagers in love. A late night ride to pick up his girl, it's easy for the listener to visualize the scene Kid Rock is setting.

"Midnight riding in my old two seater rolling down the boulevard / Headlights shining on rusty fences as I pass the rail yard / Black leather jacket on a Friday night / Fire up the lighter and a Lucky Strike," he sings on the song's first verse.

Kid Rock's gravely singing accompanied by steady percussion, wavering guitar riffs and sultry piano impress while additional backing singers gives the feel of a gospel choir. Meanwhile, the song is full of heart and soul thanks to Rock's believable singing. Additional name checks of songs by Lou Reed, Johnny Cash and Carl Perkins flush out the feel-good song.

Did You Know? Kid Rock has had several songs chart on the country charts including "All Summer Long" (No. 4), "Picture" with Sheryl Crow (No. 21), "Po-Dunk" (No. 27) and "Tennessee Mountain Top" (No. 36).
