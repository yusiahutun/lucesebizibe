SAS: Who Dares Wins star Rudy Reyes' California home with famous fiance

May 2024 · 3 minute read

SAS: Who Dares Wins has replaced Ant Middleton with former US marine and actor Rudy Reyes – and here's everything you need to know about where the star lives with his actress fiancée Jade Struck.

READ: Viewers all have same complaint about new series of SAS: Who Dares Wins

Fans may recognise Rudy's fiancée Jade Struck as she stars as Iskra in the video game Call of Duty: Modern Warfare and the couple live together in California. Fans have seen glimpses inside their Charleston apartment via Instagram – and it's a haven for their intense workouts.

Rudy Reyes' living room

After a sweaty workout session, the stars posted an impressive selfie, with Jade flexing her muscles. The image revealed the couple have a gorgeous lounge with an ornate sideboard and floral patterned armchair. The space has authentic wooden floors and is painted in a pastel blue shade.

The couple have a beautiful home

Rudy Reyes' kitchen

The star's open-plan kitchen has dark cupboards and a marble-topped island with high padded stools. Rudy set up workout bars next to his island and showed off his impressive skills on Instagram.

The star works out in his open-plan kitchen

The space was shown from another angle, revealing that there are acrobatic rings affixed to the ceiling for Rudy to perform balancing moves on. The room benefits from lots of light given the double-height windows, which have been dressed with modern blinds.

The apartment doubles as a gym

Another part of the apartment was showcased when Jade got stuck into some weighted squats. The space has a selection of unique artworks hanging on the wall, bringing some personality to the white paint. There is also a pillar in the middle of the room and wooden flooring throughout.

Rudy is father to one son

Rudy served in Afghanistan and Iraq and left the military in 2005. The star had a turbulent time growing up, being raised in foster care and he is now father to a nine-year-old son who he no longer has custody of.

Speaking to The Sun, Rudy declared: "I decided every day is about being the greatest man I can be. If my son sees me, he’ll be proud and, through his eyes, I can be proud of myself."
