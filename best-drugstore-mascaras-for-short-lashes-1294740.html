As a makeup expert, I always recommend bold and elaborate eye makeup to people with naturally short and thin lashes, to help emphasize their eyes. While this is of course not doable every day, having the best drugstore mascara for short lashes handy goes a long way! Many people believe that just using any mascara should be fine as all mascaras essentially emphasize the eyes and add definition to the lashes. However, for short and fine lashes, a mascara with a rich formula and bushy applicator can turn the eye makeup into a goopy mess instead! Hence, it is essential to invest in a product that caters to your specific makeup needs. 

An ideal mascara for short natural lashes should be able to reach the tiniest of strands with great ease, without smearing the product elsewhere or having you go through great difficulty to apply it. Such mascaras should have lengthening properties, which give a defined, fuller, and fluttery-eyed aesthetic. Moreover, if your lashes are naturally straight-ish, then the mascara should have lengthening and curling properties, so you don’t have to bother much with the eyelash curler, especially on days when you’re running late. 

Through meticulous research, trials, and testing, in addition to considering customer testimonials, I’ve cherrypicked the following OTC mascaras for their application ease, efficacy, color intensity, and lasting power. What’s more? Some of these mascaras also have nourishing formulas that replenish your eyelashes, deliver moisture, and boost growth for best results.

    Now that you’ve seen our recommendation for the best drugstore mascaras for short lashes, let’s understand how to shop for one wisely.

    How to Select the Best Mascara for Short Lashes?

    Applicator: The applicator is the key element to consider when looking for a mascara that caters to specific needs, such as lengthening short lashes, volumizing thin lashes, curling up straight lashes, etc. For short lashes, always invest in a thin brush applicator with a tapered body and fine, neatly arranged bristles. Cherry on the cake — the bristles are flexible and made with maneuverable materials such as silicone. Such applicators easily reach the tiniest of the lashes and give some extra volume and lift to the outer lashes, to spruce the aesthetic of the eyes. The fine bristles coat individual lashes with the product, for a neat look. If you also want to curl up your lashes while lengthening them, look for products with curved brushes, as they deliver the desired shape and body. 

    Consistency: Owing to the infused polymers that add length to your lashes, the consistency of such mascaras is often smooth and easy to glide. Moreover, a small quantity of such mascaras should go a long way with the brush because of their film-forming capacity. I thoroughly tested the texture and consistency of each of the above-mentioned mascaras to ensure they cater to the needs of short lashes.

    Pigment Intensity: It always helps if mascara formulations are highly pigmented as that enhances the color intensity of your eye makeup, adding some definition. Moreover, you don’t need to go in with multiple coats with such mascaras as a single glide is often bold enough. 

    Shade Options: While black is often the most sought-after shade, to cater to different preferences and skin tones, a product range should offer multiple shade options such as brown, green, blue, jet black, grey-black, light brown, hazel, etc. 

    Now, let’s understand the best ways to apply mascara for short lashes, to define and lengthen them.

    How to Apply Mascara for Short Lashes?

    Step 1: Start by dipping the mascara wand in the formula. Scrape the excess product at the opening of the tube, so we don’t over-apply or waste anything. 

    Step 2: Next, brush the applicator over the lashes, from the roots to the tips, towards the left. Repeat the same towards the right. However, avoid going back and forth more than once or twice as the mascara may dry out before we can get our desired finish.

    Step 3: After this, wiggle over the lashes with the brush in a straight, upward direction. 

    Step 4: Hold the wand there for 1-2 seconds to lift the lashes and keep the shape in place. 

    Step 5: For the lower lashes, simply wiggle over and press down with the brush. Avoid double-coating the lower lashes and ensure the emphasis is always on the upper lashes, for a balanced look.

    FAQ's

    Are drugstore mascaras as effective as high-end brands?

    They can be, depending on their formulation, efficacy, and application. Some products from high-end brands are costly only because they offer an extra touch of luxury that drugstore products may lack. The effectiveness, however, may be the same across price ranges.

    How often should I change my mascara?

    It is a good idea to throw out your mascara every six months for hygiene purposes.

    Do drugstore mascaras come with a variety of shade options?
