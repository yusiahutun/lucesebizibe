What happened to darts' biggest legends - missing teeth, tragic deaths &amp; 17-bed mansion

February 2024 · 6 minute read

He was a favourite on the darts circuit – but Andy Fordham has died aged just 59 after suffering major organ failure.

The Viking won the 2004 BDO World Darts Championship during a prestigious career but also once weighed 31st and downed 25 bottles of lager a day – before he started tackling his health issues.

His widow Jenny said: “Andy was, is and always will be my champion.”

Both former and current players paid tribute, with Bobby George branding him a “gentle giant” and “loved by all”.

But what happened to the other legends of darts? Daily Star Sport steps up to the oche to find out.

John Lowe

Along with Eric Bristow and Jocky Wilson, Lowe dominated darts during the 1980s and was one of the 16 who broke away from the BDO to form the PDC.

His clutch of titles included three world championships and he was the first player to land a televised nine-dart finish in 1984.

While he still supped booze at the oche, he took better care of his health than other stars and was also known for his sportsmanship.

His career spanned 30 years before he retired in 2007. Now 75, he is married and lives in Chesterfield, Derbys. In 2009 he penned a book, The Art of Darts. He was awarded an MBE.

Dennis Priestley

Born in Mexborough, Yorkshire, the former coal merchant won two world championships and was the first player to win both the BDO and PDC titles, in 1991 and 1994.

Dubbed “The Menace” he played up to the character by wearing red and black and using flights of those colours.

In 2007, he was diagnosed with prostate cancer but, after treatment, returned to darts the following year. Priestley retired in 2015. Now 70, he is married with four children and one grandchild.

The “Crafty Cockney” was one of the sport’s first superstars.

He won the championship five times as a player in the British Darts Organisation, dominating the sport between 1980 and 1986.

Bristow was one of the founder players when the Professional Darts Corporation was formed in 1993 as some of the sport’s leading stars broke away from the BDO. He famously suffered the mental condition ‘dartitis’ in 1986, meaning he couldn’t release the darts properly.

He downed up to 20 pints of Guinness a day, smoked and loved hot vindaloo curries.

He retired in 2007 and appeared on I’m A Celebrity in 2012.

He died in 2018 aged 60 after suffering a heart attack, the Bullseye favourite left his wife Jane and two children.

Raymond Van Barneveld

Popular Dutchman Barney spent 20 years in the BDO, winning four world championships, before switching to the PDC and scooping his fifth.

Having played since 1984, he announced his retirement in 2019 after being knocked out in the first round of the world championship – only to change his mind and make a comeback last year.

In 2019 he revealed he was to divorce wife Silvia after more than 25 years of marriage.

The 54-year-old is now dating Brit Julia Evans, 35, who lives with him in the Netherlands.

Phil Taylor

Stoke's Taylor is the richest and most successful darts player in history after winning 85 major titles and a record 16 World Championships.

Now 60, his fame translated outside darts and he has appeared in shows including Coronation Street.

“The Power” retired in 2018 but has signed to make a comeback at a new over-50s World Seniors tournament next year against former champs.

Earlier this year he was pictured getting his Covid jab at a JCB factory.

Ted Hankey

“The Count” was the pantomime villain of darts, walking on in a Dracula cloak and chucking toy bats at the audience.

He won the BDO world championship at the Lakeside in 2000 and 2009.

But the 53-year-old also once punched a dartboard in a fit of “oche rage” and in 2012 he was forced to deny being drunk on stage after looking unsteady – it later emerged he had suffered a stroke.

Dad-of-three Hankey, who lives in Telford, Shropshire, with wife Sarah has carried on playing but failed to equal his success.

Bobby George

“The King of Bling” always made an impression when he strutted onto the oche, sporting layers of jewellery plus a crown and cloak and holding a candelabra.

But he also had skill and reached two BDO world championships in 1980 and 1994.

It later emerged he had broken his back celebrating an earlier win in the 1994 tournament and he had to have eight titanium screws inserted into the base of his spine just to stand upright.

Since 1998 he has been a telly darts pundit but also appeared in a string of other shows from Celebrity Fit Club and The Real Marigold Hotel.

Now 75, he built a 17-bedroom mansion called George Hall for he and his wife Marie using skills he’d learned in the building trade in the 1960s.

He has two sons – one is also a darts player – and two grandsons.

Jocky Wilson

The Scotsman was the ultimate darts player for many, clutching a cigarette or a drink while at the oche.

Wilson said he needed seven or eight vodkas before a match to settle his nerves, and once fell off stage.

He won the world championship in 1982 and 1989, beating his great rivals John Lowe and Eric Bristow.

Wilson constantly ate sweets and refused to brush his teeth, claiming his gran had told him the “English poison the water," he had lost his last tooth by the age of 28.

He spent £1,200 on false teeth after his 1982 victory but complained they made him belch while drinking.

Once his picture ended up on Top Of The Pops behind Dexys Midnight Runners as they sang Jackie Wilson Said.

He stopped playing darts when he was 46, after being diagnosed with diabetes which prevented him from drinking during the games. He died aged 62 in 2012.
