6. Berber women of Northern Africa face paint art

The Berber women of Northern Africa paint their faces with beautiful Heena designs called siyala for their wedding events. Heena comes from dried leaves of the Henna bush and is used for many other purposes other than face painting.

The shapes and designs these women apply to their faces have cosmetic and therapeutic value. They believe that such face paintings can heal illness and fertility.

Why do African tribes paint their faces?

Body decorations have been part of African culture for numerous years. In most cases, the paintings serve as social markers, distinguishing men from boys, men from women and many more. Also, people often paint their faces for the aesthetic effect, to look more attractive.

Some paintings mark a crucial moment in a person's life, like puberty or childbirth. Others even indicate an individual's tribal heritage. All paintings have a significant meaning and vary according to the tribe; some might be about societal hierarchy, others about religious beliefs and much more.
