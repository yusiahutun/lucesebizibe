Screen star Bradley Cooper is alleged to be taking anti-baldness drug Propecia, sources claim.

According to popular US website Radar, Cooper 'is so anxious about his receding hairline' that he has resorted to the oral haircare medicine.

The 39 year-old star of American Hustle, who is currently dating Suki Waterhouse, is said to be keen to retain his leading man status - and, especially, looks.

Hair-raising: Allegations claim that Bradley is taking Propecia to prevent hair loss

Hirsute: Bradley sported thick-looking hair when he was recently in London

Currently, the popular performer sports a stylishly messy cut which he parts to one side.

The source also told People that Cooper 'has a lot of bald guys in his family, so he’s been on Propecia for a couple of years and, to his delight, it seems to be working.'

They added: 'Bradley is determined not to go bald and lose his leading man looks.'

Bradley Cooper and Suki Waterhouse at theTom Ford show in London Fashion Week

Representatives for the handsome actor, whose other film credits include The Hangover and The Place Beyond The Pines, have been contacted by MailOnline, but are yet to respond.

Propecia is a brand name of Finasteride which reverses male pattern baldness by blocking testosterone from damaging hair follicles.

Originally, it was prescribed for men with enlarged prostates, but the side-effect was soon considered a breakthrough tool to tackle hair loss.

Although many celebrities - including Jason Donavan, Wayne Rooney and James Nesbitt - have had successful hair transplants, few have been open about taking Finasteride.

Spencer Stevenson, an expert on hair loss who hosts specialist talk show The Bald Truth, recommends Propecia prior to a hair transplant - despite the fact it can have dramatic side-effects including so-called 'man-boobs' and alleged permanent erectile dysfunction.

'Before you even consider a hair transplant, look into going on Propecia or applying Minoxidil (a topical hair-loss treatment) for at least six months first, to strengthen existing hair and prevent further hair loss,' he says.
