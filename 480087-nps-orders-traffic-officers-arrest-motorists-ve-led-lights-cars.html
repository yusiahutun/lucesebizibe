NPS Orders Traffic Officers to Arrest Motorists Who've LED Lights on Their Cars

  • NPS spokesperson Bruno Shioso cited the Traffic Act (Cap 403), Section 55, read with the Traffic Rules Number 23 and 24
  • Authorities observed that several private vehicles, public service vehicles, and motorcycle owners have unlawfully LED lights
  • NPS used the opportunity to caution motorists to adhere to the rule of law at all times to ensure their safety while on the road

The National Police Service (NPS) has ordered traffic police officers to arrest all motorists who have installed LED lights on their vehicles.

In a statement, NPS spokesperson Bruno Shioso said that the move is in line with the Traffic Act (Cap 403), Section 55, read with the Traffic Rules Number 23 and 24.

"This particular section of the law provide for the requirements for the lights on vehicles (Private, PSVs, Commercial, Trailers, Trucks, Tuk Tuks and Motorcycles)," Shioso said.
