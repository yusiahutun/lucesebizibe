The disappearance of Lisa Irwin: What happened and the latest updates

February 2024 · 6 minute read

Cases of missing persons are common in most parts of the world, including the United States. Usually, when one happens, authorities launch investigations to locate the missing persons. Unfortunately, sometimes, these cases remain unsolved. One such case is the disappearance of Lisa Irwin, an infant who mysteriously vanished from her home. But what happened? And was Lisa ever found?

What happened to Baby Lisa? That is the mystery surrounding the disappearance of Lisa Irwin. Police have not been able to locate her since she vanished from her house in Kansas City, Missouri. Her case has drawn national and international attention, with many wondering about her whereabouts.

Who is Lisa Irwin?

Lisa Renée Irwin was a 10-month-old baby who vanished from her home in Kansas City, Missouri, on 3 October 2011. Her mother last saw her on the evening of her disappearance at around 10 pm.

Lisa was born on 11 November 2010 to Deborah Bradley and Jeremy Irwin. She has two older brothers. At the time of her disappearance, Lisa was ten months and 23 days old.

Disappearance of Lisa Irwin

Here is a timeline detailing the events leading to the disappearance of Lisa Irwin, according to Uncovered.

3 October 2011

At around 2:30 pm, Jeremy Irwin returned home from his day job as an electrician. He hangs out with his children while their mother gets some rest. At 4:45, Deborah and her brother Phillip head to a supermarket to buy baby food and boxed wine.

At around 5 pm, Deborah and her brother returned home from shopping. At 5:30 pm, Jeremy leaves for a night job at a nearby Starbucks. Phillip also departs, leaving his sister with the next-door neighbour, Samantha Brando.

At 6 pm, Samantha heads out to purchase more alcohol for the night. She returns at around 6:30 pm, and ten minutes later, at around 6:40 pm, Deborah puts the baby (Lisa) in her crib. Between around 6:40 pm and 10 pm, Deborah and Samantha sit on the porch drinking and smoking while the children play inside.

At around 10:30 pm, Deborah heads to sleep, leaving three cell phones on the counter and turning off the lights. Her sons fall asleep in her bed while Baby Lisa's door remains closed. Their neighbour, Samantha, stays on the porch until 11:30 pm, conversing with another neighbour.

4 October 2011

At around 3:30 am, Jeremy Irwin returns from his night job and finds the house lights on. He also discovers damage on the screen, his front door unlocked, and the computer room window pushed in. Jeremy rushes to check on his family, noticing that Lisa is not in her crib.

He wakes up Deborah to ask the whereabouts of their daughter. They search around the house and call 911 after failing to find her.

Lisa Irwin's investigation

Following Lisa's disappearance, police began a multiple law enforcement agency investigation. They discovered that somebody used one of Deborah's cell phones to call a person known as Megan Wright. However, Megan didn't answer her phone that night.

As a result of the discovery, police dug into one year's worth of Deborah and Jeremy's phone records but found no known connection to Megan Wright.

Witness accounts

Two witnesses allegedly claimed to authorities to have seen a man walking down the street with a baby. A motorist also reported seeing a man walking alone with a baby at around 4 am.

Polygraph test

On 7 October 2011, authorities conducted a polygraph test on Deborah as she was the last person seen with the child. The police, as an interrogation tactic, reportedly told her that she had failed the test to try to convince her to talk.

Home Search

According to KMBC News, on 19 October 2011, FBI agents searched the Irwin home. Court documents showed that the FBI agents took many items of evidence from the house. They confiscated a multicolour comforter, purple shorts, a multicolour Disney character shirt, a Glo Worm toy, a "Cars"-themed blanket, rolls of tape and a tape dispenser.". They also took carpet samples and dug up the backyard.

Was Lisa Irwin found alive?

No. Lisa is still missing. As of November 2023, the disappearance of Lisa Irwin has yielded no result, and she hasn't been found alive. The FBI is still searching for her, having been missing for over 12 years (as of the time of writing).

Are Lisa Irwin's parents guilty?

No, they are not. As of November 2023, Lisa Irwin's parents have not been charged or found guilty of the disappearance of their daughter in 2011. However, media speculation around the character of Deborah for getting drunk with a baby in the house has led to many theories about the case.

Are Baby Lisa Irwin's parents divorced?

There is no mention of Lisa Irwin's parents divorcing. According to Daily Mail, the couple were engaged at the time of their daughter's disappearance, and there has been no mention of whether they married since. Furthermore, details emerged that Deborah was still married to her first husband and had not divorced but had separated four years before the events of 2011.

Final word on the disappearance of Lisa Irwin

The disappearance of Lisa Irwin has remained a mystery for over a decade. Despite extensive investigations and searches, she is still missing. The case has drawn national attention and continues to perplex investigators.
