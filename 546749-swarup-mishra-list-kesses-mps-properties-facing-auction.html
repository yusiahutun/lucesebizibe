"Under instructions from our client, we shall sell the following properties by public auction," the notice by Jomuki Auctioneers read.

Why were Swarup Mishra's properties auctioned?

The properties are registered under Mediheal Group Limited, owned by Mishra and his wife, Pallavi Jodhpur Rajasthan.

Jomuki Auctioneers revealed to TUKO.co.ke that Mishra had accumulated debts from banks running into millions of shillings.

"Mishra is owed by several banks. I cannot reveal the amount. It is true that we have put several of his properties on sale and the auction is set for Tuesday, July 2, 2024," Jomuki Auctioneers told TUKO.co.ke.

Which properties owned by Mishra were put on sale?

  • Piece of land (0.2223 acres) in Pioneer/Ngeria within Blossom area, Kiambaa-Kesses division.
  • Three pieces of land in Cheptiret/Cheplaskei measuring 10, 30.6 and 10 acres at Ngeria centre.
  • Land unit in Eldoret municipality block 14/474 measuring 1.076 acres in Elgon View-Greenvale area.
  • Land unit in Eldoret municipality block 14/475 measuring 1.086 acres in Elgon View-Greenvale area.

What were the conditions for the sale?

Interested bidders were requested to view the property and confirm they were the ones put on sale.

Bidders were required to pay a refundable KSh 100,000 to obtain bidding numbers.

"Sale is subject to reserve price, land board control consent and other overriding interests where applicable," Jomuki Auctioneers explained.

What is Swarup Mishra's net worth?

When the Eldoret businessman came to Kenya from India in 1997 with only KSh 50,000, he didn't imagine he would create a multi-billion empire.

According to Business Today, Mishra created a business empire worth KSh 10 billion before entering politics and running into debt.

Since he and his wife were medical doctors, they sought new opportunities in Kenya owing to the highly competitive field in India.

The duo settled in Eldoret, now one of the fastest-growing towns in Kenya.

The ex-Kesses MP recalled his wife secured a job ahead of him, and they had to survive on her salary.

Mishra moved from town to town to make ends meet, conducting surgeries before being appointed a lecturer at Moi University's Department of Reproductive Health.

He taught medical students for over 16 years and made savings.

How Mishra and his wife developed Mediheal Hospital

In 2006, they acquired Pacifica Hospital, located along Nandi Road, Eldoret and renamed it Mediheal Hospital.

The two renovated the health facility, converting it into an obstetrics and fertility centre with 150 beds.

Besides Mediheal Group of Hospitals, Mishra has invested in the real estate sector in Eldoret.

Mishra, called 'Kiprop arap Chelule' by locals, was elected Kesses MP in the 2017 General Election on a Jubilee Party ticket. He lost the seat in 2022 after falling out with President William Ruto.

Inside Mishra's palatial house

The embattled businessman launched his palatial mansion in 2020.

Outside the main house, which could be mistaken for a state lodge, Mishra's compound includes three other impressive buildings.
