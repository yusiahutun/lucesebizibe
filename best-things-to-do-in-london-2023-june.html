40 joyful things to do in London in June 2023

June 2024 · 31 minute read

Fill up your social schedule with HELLO!'s list of restaurants, rooftop bars, free events, and more in the capital this month

    May might have brought spring blooms and sunnier skies to London, but as we head into June, the start of summer is in sight and a whole host of rooftop bars, alfresco dining spots and open air activities await city dwellers looking for things to do in the capital. 

    London is host to an array of joyful popups, new restaurant openings, family-friendly events and colourful weekend activities to fill up your calendar. From immersive art installations to bottomless brunches, outdoor theatre and sky-high dining, it doesn't get much better than sundowners in the city.

    Best activities to do in London in June

    Hit up the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic at the Hurlingham Club

    The Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic is returning to the pristine grounds of the Hurlingham Club between 27 June – 1 July with a star-studded lineup. If you're looking for a pre-Wimbledon warmup to round off the month,  the Hurlingham Club promises both luxury hospitality and top tier tennis. For only the second time in the event’s history, tickets are now on sale to the general public to attend on Saturday 1 July starting at £185, with hospitality tickets available during the week from £595. 

    New for 2023, the Saturday programme will feature two top ATP singles matches and a legends match, offering attendees an intimate sporting experience amongst some of the greatest players to grace the courts. Norwegian superstar and current No. 4 in the world, Casper Ruud, is the first name to be added to the line-up with more top players to be announced in the coming weeks.

    Saturday tickets include: a grandstand seat on centre court; complimentary Whispering Angel rosé and a variety of other wines, beers & soft drinks for the duration of the session (13.30 - 17.30); access to the exclusive Whispering Angel Garden bar; and access to a variety of gourmet food options.

    For tickets, visit www.giorgioarmanitennisclassic.seetickets.com

    Pick up a designer bargain at the O2

    Icon Outlet Shopping at The O2 is a total shopaholic's playground, with everything from highstreet labels to designer brands, homeware stores and beauty outlets offering major savings of up to 70% off everything in stock. Located inside London's O2 and moments walk from North Greenwich tube station, Icon Outlet Shopping is the ultimate pre-concert pitstop or post-show bargain hunt for those visiting The O2.

    On Thursday 1 and Friday 2 June, shoppers will be able to book the ultimate dream team of retail accomplices ahead of shopping trips to The O2, who will not only ensure their shopping needs are fulfilled but keep spirits high and shoppers motivated throughout the duration of their trip. The free 'Hire a Hype Squad' service is bookable in advance and will last 90 minutes – allowing plenty of time to source must-have items from over 60 brands, including Levi’s, Nike, Reiss, Superdry, and more.

    Get the royal treatment at Stanely's Chelsea for Royal Ascot

    From Tuesday 20 - Saturday 24 June, Stanley's Chelsea is being transformed into Stanley's Enclosure for all equestrian fans and Ascot lovers alike wishing to immerse themselves over the course of the grand affair. Enjoy all the action from the track while you dine, with screens placed throughout the restaurant.

    If you're keen to enjoy the Royal Ascot experience, tickets are £75 per person, which will include a welcome drink from Whispering Angel, a three-course meal and a quintessentially British afternoon tea which can be enjoyed whilst watching all the sporting fun.

    To book, visit www.stanleyschelsea.co.uk

Adding to their array of interactive entertainment, the experience combines Augmented Reality and cutting-edge technology in three unique games focusing on action, puzzle and skill.     To book, visit allstarlanes.co.uk

    A night at the cabaret

    The hot summer nights are officially here and how better to enjoy them than at the London Cabaret Club, which are bringing Brazilian beach vibes to The Bloomsbury Ballroom with their new show 'Copacabana'. Set over three distinctive acts, the new show guides audiences through a trio of concepts based around the 'Copacabana' theme, beginning on the sun-drenched shores of Rio De Janeiro.

    Act two focuses on the world-renowned New York City nightclub Copacabana, notorious for its celebrity clientele that featured early performances from the likes of Dean Martin and Jerry Lewis – a much fabled setting used in a plethora of award-winning films. Act three introduces 'Lola The Showgirl' – the Latin dancer immortalised in Barry Manilow's iconic 'Copacabana' theme tune. 

    Guests will enjoy tropical cocktails and a unique Brazilian menu, which will be served during the show to perfectly complement the atmosphere

    Visit Lowri Cooper's first solo exhibition at The Roundhouse

    Head to The Roundhouse in Camden to uncover some stunning photographic work by photographer Lowri Cooper. Cooper is a Welsh visual artist based in London, specialising in fashion photography. Her solo exhibition showcases a selection of still and moving-image works, charting the photographer‘s personal journey and collaborations to date. The collection represents a journey through Cooper‘s artistic story and their experimental approach to work, questioning what a fashion image can be by reshaping old forms and creating new ones.

    The works explore core themes of nature, freedom, strength, beauty, and artificiality through portraiture, scanography, sound, moving-image, and fashion photography; boldly addressing colour and its absence through monochrome palettes and complimentary tones inspired by the natural world.

    For more info, visit eventbrite.co.uk
