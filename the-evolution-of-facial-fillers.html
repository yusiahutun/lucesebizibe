The evolution of facial fillers

September 2024 · 3 minute read

This content has been created in collaboration with Juvéderm.

Image courtesy of Juvéderm

Collagen-based fillers became popular in the 1980s, after the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA) approved their use in 1981. While the cosmetic procedure has had a stigma attached to it, nowadays it is possible – with the right practitioner and a trusted brand of facial filler – to achieve subtle, sophisticated results. There are two key reasons for this: scientific advances in the formula and refined application techniques.

With the help of two experts, Bazaar explores the innovations that are changing the reputation of this treatment…

The art of application

The approach to filler has changed a lot over the past 10 years. While the focus used to be on specific lines and creases, today it is more about looking at the face as a whole and the underlying cause of ageing, such as loss of volume. The medical director of the Phi Clinic, Dr Tapan Patel follows the MD Code, which was developed by the Brazilian plastic surgeon Mauricio de Maio. "This concept allows a practitioner to assess the whole face and determine the impact of treating one area and how that might also have a positive impact on an adjacent area," explains Dr Tapan. "Augmenting the cheek may also lift the lower face tissue, thereby better defining the jawline."

While this is a game-changing technique, which allows practitioners to provide subtle definition and volume to the face, it also highlights the key point that finding the right doctor for you is absolutely essential to getting the results you want.

The innovative formulation

"In my eyes, Allergan, with the Juvéderm hyaluronic acid range, has been at the forefront of advancing dermal filler science," says the cosmetic surgeon Dr Benji Dhillon. "They have continuously innovated by focusing on some important elements, which make the products more predictable." Juvéderm's most recent range, Vycross, offers three different products that are tailored to specific areas of your face. The first, Voluma, provides general volume and lift, while Volbella is slightly gentler to soften the appearance of ageing around your eyes. The third product, Volift, gives mild volume to lips and skin folds by the corners of the mouth and nose.
