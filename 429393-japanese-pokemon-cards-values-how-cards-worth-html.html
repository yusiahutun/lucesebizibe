Japanese Pokmon cards values: How much are your cards worth?

April 2024 · 7 minute read

Anime sequels such as Dragon Ballz and Pokemon have been in the anime industry for over a decade and have amassed a substantial fan base worldwide. However, the anime Pokemon proprietors took over the sector after introducing character cards that fans craved for. As a result, Japanese Pokemon cards values have skyrocketed and some are worth a lumpsum.

The Pokemon Trading card game was introduced in October 1996 in Japan by the producing firm, media factory. It entails Pokemon cards where two players of all ages battle. The victor is the one with the most valuable card. The cards vary from Pokemon cards, Trainer cards to Energy cards. Stick around to find out the worth of Japanese Pokemon cards.

How can you tell if a Japanese card is rare?

There are several ways to find out how rare a Japanese card is worth. For example,

  • A marker near the bottom of the card or close to its name will indicate rarity
  • A circle means the card is standard.
  • A diamond means it’s less common.
  • Stars showcase how rare the card is.

In addition, other characteristics that can boost the rarity include: a higher printed number than there should be in a given printed set (e.g., 67/66) and holographic artwork or reverse holographic in a Japanese card. Furthermore, a card that bears the first edition mark on the side denotes it is from the first run of cards in the set. Therefore, giving the Pokemon card extra value.

What are the most valuable Japanese Pokemon cards?

There are pretty several Japanese Pokemon cards that are in use. However, not all carry the same value. So what Japanese cards are worth money? The following are some of the Pokemon cards that have the highest value in the market.

1. 1999 First Edition Shadowless Holographic Charizard

Top on the list of Pokemon cards worth money is this card. What makes this card all peculiar is the lack of shadow underneath the fire-breathing dragon Pokemon. However, this was a printing error which was later rectified in other printed cards making it even rarer. The card was sold at an auction in October 2020 for $220,574 to rapper Logic setting a record.

2. Pokémon Blastoise #009/165R Commissioned Presentation Galaxy Star Hologram

The ultra-rare Pokémon card was created in 1998 as a presentation piece by Magic: The Gathering maker Wizards of the Coast. They aimed to convince the Pokeman Japan executives to allow them to handle the game’s English-language release.

There are only two kinds of Pokemon cards in existence, one of which was sold for $360,000 at an auction in January 2021. The location of the other card remains a mystery, meaning it may be the only of its kind left.

3. Black Star Ishihara Signed GX Promo Card

The card depicts Pokémon Company founder and current president Tsunekazu Ishihara. Tsunekazu offered it to the company staff to celebrate his 60th birthday back in 2017. However, this specific version is rarer since Ishihara signed this card to boost its price even further. The Black Star card was sold for nearly a quarter of a million dollars on April 26 2021.

4. Pikachu Illustrator

The games organizers awarded the Japanese card to winners of promo contests held in 1997 and 1998. Pikachu Illustrator is among the rarest Pokémon cards since it is the only Pokemon card to say Illustrator instead of Trainer at the top of the card.

Moreover, it has an iconic one-off pen in its bottom-right corner acknowledging its design for the contest. It was sold for a whopping $195,000 at an auction in 2019.

5. Kangaskhan-Holo #115 Family Event Trophy Card

It is the third-rarest card in history since it has never been mass-produced. However, the card was given as a trophy card at a family contest. It included the Pocket Monsters Card Game logo and the original Japanese name for the Pokémon on the back and front of the card as a trophy card. A copy was sold on eBay in October 2020 for over $150,000.

6. 2000 Pokémon Neo Genesis 1st Edition Holo Lugia #9

As a legendary bird Pokemon from Gen II, Lugia is one of the most potent and hard-to-find Pokémon cards adding to its popularity. There are claims that 41 of these cards exist, but only three meet the threshold grade. A thoroughly graded copy of the card was sold at auction for $144,000 in May 2021.

7. 1999 Super Secret Battle No. 1 Trainer

No. 1 Trainer is a holographic promotional card awarded to finalists in the Secret Super Battle tournament held in Tokyo, Japan, in 1999. It was awarded to regional finalists, and by presenting the card, the finalists gained preferential entry into the Secret Super Battle.

It is believed that at the time, the organizers made only seven cards. In addition, the grading organization PSA graded six of the cards to be of good quality. As such, a quality card was sold at auction in July 2020 for $90,000.

8. 1999 Pokémon Japanese Promo Tropical Mega Battle Tropical Wind

Only twelve tropical wind cards were produced as promotional cards for the 1999 Tropical Mega battle. Fifty players from around the globe took part in the tournament for the trading card game in Honolulu, Hawaii.

A contender had to fight to the final round to participate, and the card was offered as a prize, making them extremely rare. The most recent record-breaking sale took place in October 2020, with an estimated value of $148,842.

9. 2002 Pokémon World Championships No. 1 Trainer

The 2002 No. 1 Trainer card was customized with the name of the tournament’s winner, making each card unique. Regional tournaments were held in preparation for the Pokemon World Championships, with the cards as prizes to the winners. This card is scarce, and a copy was sold at an auction in April 2021 for over $31,000.

10. Espeon and Umbreon Gold Star POP Series 5

The cards are named by a gold star near the Pokémon’s name at the top of the card. Moreover, this signifies that the artwork was different from the standard version. Twenty-seven gold cards were produced between 2004 and 2007, making them some of the rarest Pokemon cards.

For example, a Gold Star Espeon was graded and sold for $22,000 in February 2021. In contrast, an Umbreon garnered over $20,000 in December 2020.
