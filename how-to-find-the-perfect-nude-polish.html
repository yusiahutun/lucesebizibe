How to Find the Perfect Nude Polish

September 2024 Â· 2 minute read

Question: How do I find the most flattering shade of nude nail polish for my skin tone?

Answer: If you feel like there are a million beiges and browns on the market and still not a single one that works for you, you're not alone. Backstage at the Spring 2016, manicurists acknowledged time and time again how tricky it was to get the naked nail just right. "It's so hard to find a nude that looks good, but I find it's best to start out with a color that's super opaque and mix it with 1/10 of a bottle of top coat to sheer it out and create your own custom blend," said Miss Pop, who did just that at Rodarte using O.P.I. Nail Lacquer in A Great Opera-Tunity, a peachy shade that becomes "natural and the perfect fall neutral" when diluted with a top coat. Zoya Color Developer Rebecca Isa agrees that "finding the exact match from a bottle doesn't happen very often because there is just such a wide range of skin tones" and takes a similar DIY approach. First, she says, look at the undertones of your skin the way you would if you were choosing blush and match according to whether you're warm or cool. Then, decide where you want to fall on the natural to mannequin scale. "For a full-on mannequin hand, go a little deeper and darker for more depth, and for a softer finish that looks like an extension of the skin, go a shade lighter than your foundation shade."
