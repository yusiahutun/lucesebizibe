Blow to William Ruto as High Court Declines to Set Aside Orders Suspending Finance Act 2023

August 2024 Â· 2 minute read
  • High Court Judge Mugure Thande said the issues raised by the petitioner are weighty and need to be heard by an expanded bench
  • Thande directed that the Finance Act 2023 suit be forwarded to Chief Justice Martha Koome for further direction
  • Consequently, Justice Thande declined to lift the conservatory orders suspending the implementation of the contentious act

Nairobi - Milimani High Court Judge Mugure Thande has dismissed the plea by President William Ruto's government to have conservatory orders against the Finance Act 2023 lifted.

In her ruling, Justice Thande argued that if the orders are lifted, then there's danger in rendering the case an academic exercise.

Martha Koome to appoint a 3-judge bench

She argued that lifting the conservatory orders would be to the public, as people would be subjected to unconstitutional laws.

While referring the matter to Chief Justice Martha Koome to form a three-judge bench, Thande ruled that the petitioners had raised serious constitutional issues that required determination by more than one judge.

"The petitioners have proved that they have a prima facie case....there's merit in granting conservatory orders," the judge noted.

Consequently, Justice Thande extended orders suspending the implementation of the Finance Act 2023 until the case by Busia Senator Okiya Omtatah and six others is heard and determined.

Why Omtatah suit Kenya Kwanza government

Omtatah had filed a petition challenging the Act, arguing that it was unconstitutional and infringed on numerous rights.

After the National Assembly passed the bill, the seasoned civil rights activists amended his petition and highlighted sections that needed to be scrapped.

In the new petition, Omtatah claimed the Speaker of the National Assembly, Moses Wetang'ula, violated Article 110(3) of the Constitution by introducing the Finance Bill 2023 in the National Assembly without first determining, jointly with the Speaker of the Senate Amason Kingi, whether the bill affected devolved units.

The Busia senator had also contested the 8% VAT increment on petroleum products by the Kenya Kwanza government.
