CMT Music Awards best moments: From Gwen Stefani and Shania Twain to Carrie Underwood

February 2024 · 9 minute read

Country music's biggest night of the year is here, with the 2023 CMT Music Awards taking place in Austin, Texas. The show kicked off with an incredibly emotional pre-recorded speech from host Kelsea Ballerini, who broke down in tears, as she paid tribute to "the ever growing list of friends, family, survivors, witnesses and responders whose lives contineue to be forever changed by gun violence". She began by honoring the three students and three staff members who were killed at random on March 27, 2023 at The Covenant School in Nashville. 

"The communtiy of sorrow over this and the 130 mass hooting sin the US this year alone stretches from coast to coast," she continued, before sharing the heartbreaking story of how in 2008 she watched her castmate lose his life "to a gun in our cafeteria"

Carly Pearce showed off her rock side with an incredible performance of No Doubt's 'Just A Girl' with Gwen Stefani, while Ingrid Andress, Lainey Wilson, Madeline Edward and Morgan Wade joined Alanis Morisette for a stunning performance of Alanis' iconic song 'You Oughta Know'.

Lainey - who was nominated for four awards - picked up two, while Jelly Roll was the big winner of the night taking home all three awards for which he was nominated.

The ceremony aired on the CBS Television Network and it's also available to stream  on-demand on Paramount+. Fans who tuned in early saw the likes of Carrie Underwood, LeAnn Rimes and Shania Twain rock the red carpet in a series of gorgeous looks.

This is how you close out a country show!

Wynonna Judd! LeAnn Rimes! ZZ Top's Billy Gibbons! Guns n Roses' Slash! Cody Johnson! Allman Brothers' Warren Haynes and Chuck Leavell! Bad Company frontman Paul Rodgers!

The mega group of performers came together to perform a tribute to Lynard Skynard. The tribute featured two of the band’s biggest hits - including 'Sweet Home Alabama' - and comes a few weeks after the death of Gary Rossington, Lynyrd Skynyrd’s final original founding member and guitarist.

Kane Brown and Katelyn Brown win Video of the Year

And the final award of the night, Video of the Year, goes to Kane Brown and Katelyn Brown for 'Thank God'.

"This is all so new to me and when we recorded this a year ago I never would have dreamed this would happen," said Katelyn before thanking her "amazing husband" Kane.

"Thank you to the fans who voted, Sony and my manager Martha," added Kane, before thanking the director Alex Alvga, who had moved from Italy and was living "the American dream".

Kane and Katelyn make CMT performance debut 

Kane Brown and wife Katelyn married in 2018 and have two kids - and are now making their CMT debut as a duet! They worked together on the hit ''Thank God,'  which number one on the Country Airplay chart in February 2023, making it the second duet by a married couple to top the chart.

The first? Country royalty Tim McGraw and Faith Hill.

Shania accepts CMT Equal Play Award

Megan is here to introduce the CMT Equal Play Award winner - the one and only Shania Twain!

The award celebrates artists who are advocates for “elevating diverse and underrepresented voices in country music,” according to the CMTs. She is the third artist to receive the honor, whose previous winners were Jennifer Nettles and Linda Martell.

"When I wrote the phrase 'Man I feel like a woman,' I had no idea it would become an undercurrent of a decades-long career," said Shania. "It's amazing how just one statement can empower so many." 

Shania - who is wearing a very daring purple crop top and micro mini shorts - added: "I promise I will continue to champion the many country artists who are not played, streamed or awarded to the level they deserve."

"I will continue to do my best as a trailblazer," she concluded.

Wynonna Judd calls 2023 ceremony 'bittersweet'

"It's a bittersweet day. CMT has been a large part ofour lives... I had no idea it would be the last time I would sing with my mother," Wynonna shared on the red carpet. 

Wynonna and her late mom Naomi performed 'Love Can Build A Bridge' at the 2022 ceremony. Naomi died by suicide in April 2022, two weeks earlier. It was mother-daughter duo's first televised appearance as The Judds in over 20 years. 

Carrie Underwood rocks the red carpet solo

"My husband has the kiddos tonight," Carrie shared on the red carpet. 

The superstar looked gorgeous in a silver sequin romper with matching stiletto heels. Carrie is married to former NHL star Mike Fisher and they have two sons.

Kelsea breaks silence over her red carpet guest

"I have a really hot date tonight," said Kelsea, when asked about her guest, Chase Stokes. 

Kelsea confirmed their relationship during an appearance on Today after co-anchor, Sheinelle Jones, told viewers: "For those of you who don't know, they're dating." 

Chase and Kelsea were first spotted together in January 2023 when they attended a football game. The actor shared a photo of them cuddled up in their seats, and while it didn't reveal their faces, he did tag Kelsea in the picture.  A few weeks later, Chase alluded to their relationship, telling TMZ: "She's a sweet girl. We're having a good time and that's all I'll say." 

The red carpet is actually... aqua?

The carpet may not have been red so it looks like the celebrities took control! 

Red was the color of the night on the carpet, with Lainey and Shania rocking stunning fits.

Katelyn Jae to make awards debut

Katelyn Jae, Kane Brown's wife, is looking classy in a pure white column dress with long white gloves. "I am used to watching Kane do his thing so I feel blessed to be doing this with my husband," she said on the red carpet when asked about their upcoming performance!
